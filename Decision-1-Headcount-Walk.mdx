<Tooltip tip="Meridian Staffing is a fictional 13-person RPO specializing in U.S. federal government hiring, used throughout this guide to demonstrate RequisitionIQ." headline="Who is Meridian Staffing?">Meridian Staffing</Tooltip> has 29 open reqs for Forest Services and needs to know **where their client's headcount will land on February 1st**, since the client has a major project initiative they need staffed.

RequisitionIQ uses a <Tooltip tip="A step-by-step calculation that projects ending headcount by starting with current headcount and applying known workforce changes, such as transfers, terminations, accepted offers, and predicted requisition fill dates.">headcount waterfall</Tooltip> and a <Tooltip tip="A statistical model that estimates the probability an open requisition will fill by a future date based on historical hiring patterns and the requisition's characteristics.">survival model</Tooltip> to predict the likelihood  open reqs will fill by the target end date.

**The output is the predicted fill model below.**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Current HC">
    Total number of active employees in the organization you want to model as of Current HC Date and is the start of the headcount walk.
  </Step>
  <Step title="IN">
    Transfers of active employees from one organization to another within the Current and Ending HC Date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="OUT">
    Active employees moving from the organization you selected to a different organization by the Ending HC Date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Terms">
    Future terminations the will be successfully completed by the Ending HC Date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Offers Accepted">
    All offers that have a Start Date between the Current and Ending HC Date. Offers that are "past due" are excluded and require their start date updated to be included in the model.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Predicted Fills">
    RequisitionIQ deploys the COX Survival Model to predict when open requisition will fill by the Ending HC Date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ending HC">
    The forecasted ending headcount with a confidence level of 95%.
  </Step>
</Steps>
