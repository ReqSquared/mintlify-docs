<Tooltip tip="Meridian Staffing is a fictional 13-person RPO specializing in U.S. federal government hiring, used throughout this guide to demonstrate RequisitionIQ." headline="Who is Meridian Staffing?">Meridian Staffing</Tooltip> has 29 open reqs for Forest Services and needs to know **where their client's headcount will land on February 1st**, since the client has a major project initiative they need staffed.

RequisitionIQ uses a <Tooltip tip="A step-by-step calculation that projects ending headcount by starting with current headcount and applying known workforce changes, such as transfers, terminations, accepted offers, and predicted requisition fill dates.">headcount waterfall</Tooltip> and a <Tooltip tip="A statistical model that estimates the probability an open requisition will fill by a future date based on historical hiring patterns and the requisition's characteristics.">survival model</Tooltip> to predict the likelihood  open reqs will fill by the target end date.

**The output is the predicted fill waterfall model below.**

## Detailed Walkthrough

<Card title="How do you go from raw ATS Data to Predicting Fills?" icon="cloud-question">
  Read below for a quick guide on RequisitionIQ's Decision Object #1: The Headcount Walk.
</Card>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Filled Requisitions">
    <Frame>
      ![Headcount Walk Step 3 No Header](/images/headcount_walk_step_3_no_header.png)
    </Frame>

    We start with every Forest Service job we've already filled. RequisitionIQ defaults to the recruiter, location, role, and how many days each one took to fill. **This history is what the model learns from.**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Survival Model">
    <Frame>
      ![Headcount Walk Step 4 No Header](/images/headcount_walk_step_4_no_header.png)
    </Frame>

    The model studies those past fill times and learns the typical timeline: how a job's odds of being filled climb day by day. From rare in the first few weeks to near-certain by  day 90.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Predicting Fills (Open Requisitions)">
    <Frame>
      ![Headcount Walk Step 5 No Header](/images/headcount_walk_step_5_no_header.png)
    </Frame>

    Then it places each  open job on the learned timeline based on how long it's already been open, and scores its chance of filling by February 1st. For Forest Service, 6 of 29 open requisitions clear the bar. **21% are predicted to fill by February 1st.**
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Next Steps

Now that the Headcount Walk is complete, we can step back and decide what action to take.\
\
With **6 open requisitions predicted to fill** and **12 employees expected to leave or transfer out of Forest Services**, the client team can determine whether the organization is still on track to hit its February 1st headcount target.\
\
If a gap remains, the next step is **Decision Object #2: Requisition Personas**, where RequisitionIQ evaluates the remaining **23 open requisitions** to identify where recruiting effort can be accelerated.
